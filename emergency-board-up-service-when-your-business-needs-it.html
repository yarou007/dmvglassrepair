Emergency Board-Up Service: When Your Business Needs It

Emergency board-up is not just for major disasters. It is often the fastest way to protect a business entrance, storefront, or window after unexpected breakage.

What to Do First

Board-up is critical when openings are exposed to weather, theft risk, or pedestrian hazards. Immediate secure-up helps protect staff, customers, and inventory while replacement materials are arranged.

Repair vs Replacement Considerations

If the frame and surrounding system are stable, replacement can happen quickly. If hardware, framing, or code upgrades are needed, planning the full scope early helps avoid repeated downtime.

Commercial Planning Tips

Businesses benefit from one vendor who can secure the site and complete final replacement. This reduces handoff delays and improves accountability across emergency and scheduled phases.

Response Time

Prioritize vendors that can secure openings quickly and communicate expected arrival windows clearly.

Scope Clarity

Ask for clear estimates with materials, labor, and any code-related requirements before approval.

Property Coordination

Use teams experienced with tenants, retail operations, and after-hours scheduling to reduce disruption.
