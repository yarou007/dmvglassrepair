Tempered Glass vs Laminated Glass for Storefronts

Storefront owners often ask whether tempered or laminated glass is the better choice. The right answer depends on safety goals, security needs, and budget.

What to Do First

Tempered glass is strong and commonly required in many door and storefront applications. It breaks into smaller pieces, which can reduce injury risk in impact events.

Repair vs Replacement Considerations

Laminated glass adds an interlayer that can hold fragments together and improve security performance. It may be preferred where break-in resistance and noise control are priorities.

Commercial Planning Tips

Selection should consider code requirements, exposure level, and operating conditions. Clear recommendations from your installer help avoid costly rework.

Response Time

Prioritize vendors that can secure openings quickly and communicate expected arrival windows clearly.

Scope Clarity
