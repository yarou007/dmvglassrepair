Emergency Board-Up vs Glass Replacement: What Comes First?

If your storefront glass breaks in Washington DC, Maryland, or Northern Virginia, the first decision affects safety, liability, and how quickly you reopen.

Start with Safety and Site Security

If glass is shattered, missing, or leaves direct access to the property, board-up usually comes first. It protects people, secures inventory, and reduces weather exposure while replacement is scheduled.

Need immediate secure-up? Call 703-244-0559 for 24/7 emergency board-up service.

When Direct Replacement Can Happen First

Direct replacement can be possible when the opening is stable, access is controlled, and the required glass type is available quickly. This is more common for standard door lites or smaller panels with known specs.

  • Tempered vs laminated requirements affect lead time
  • Custom sizes and thicker units usually require board-up first
  • After-hours work often starts with temporary secure-up

What to Send for Faster Decisions

To reduce delays, send photos from inside and outside, approximate width/height, business name, exact location, and whether the opening faces public sidewalk traffic.

Quick Takeaway for Property Managers

In most emergency cases, secure the opening first, then replace with the correct glass. This sequence lowers risk and usually keeps approvals cleaner for tenant-facing sites.
